# Mula Group — Copywriting Guide

**Version:** 1.0.0
**Last updated:** 2026-07-05
**Language:** Polish (primary), English (secondary, for international pages)

---

## 1. Brand Voice

Mula Group is a technology ecosystem. We are direct, confident, and focused on business outcomes.

**What we sound like:**

- **Direct** — No hedging, no softening qualifiers. We say what we do and what the result will be.
- **Confident** — We know our craft. We do not "suggest" or "propose" — we design, we deploy, we deliver.
- **Technical but accessible** — Use precise terminology where it adds clarity, but never to impress. A CEO should understand every sentence without a translator.
- **Outcome-obsessed** — Every sentence answers: "What does this mean for the client's business?"
- **Polish-first** — Natural, contemporary Polish business language. Avoid archaic formalisms.

**What we do NOT sound like:**

- Corporate brochure ("Naszą misją jest dostarczanie innowacyjnych rozwiązań...")
- Buzzword generator ("synergia", "leveraging", "disrupting")
- Agency pitch ("kreatywny", "unikalny", "kompleksowy" — unless backed by specific proof)
- Hype machine ("rewolucyjny", "game-changer", "następna generacja")

## 2. Headline Formula

### Primary Formula

```
[Co robimy] — [Wartość dla klienta]
```

The dash separates action from outcome. The first part names the capability. The second part answers "why should I care?"

**Examples:**

- "Projektujemy systemy AI — automatyzacja procesów, które zajmują Twój zespół"
- "Wdrażamy e-commerce — sprzedaż wielokanałowa bez chaosu operacyjnego"
- "Audyt cyberbezpieczeństwa — pełna zgodność z NIS2 w 30 dni"

### Secondary Formula (for section headers)

```
[Czasownik działania] + [przedmiot działania] + [kontekst branżowy]
```

**Examples:**

- "Automatyzujemy procesy w dystrybucji B2B"
- "Projektujemy hale stalowe dla przemysłu"
- "Wdrażamy strategie marketingowe dla e-commerce"

### Rules

1. Headlines are one sentence. No period at end.
2. Max 12 words in Polish (compound nouns count as one).
3. No passive voice. Agent (we) is implicit or explicit.
4. Never start with "Nasza firma..." or "Mula Group jest..."
5. Use numbers where credible: "w 30 dni", "7 filarów", "200+ realizacji"

## 3. CTA Formula

### Primary Formula

```
[Czasownik działania] + [rzeczownik wartości] + [ramy czasowe / natychmiastowość]
```

**Examples:**

- "Porozmawiajmy o projekcie"
- "Zamów bezpłatną konsultację w 24h"
- "Sprawdź jak to działa"
- "Odbierz audyt procesów"
- "Zacznij automatyzację jutro"

### CTA Placement Rules

1. **Above the fold** — One primary CTA in the hero. Links to contact or next section.
2. **After value delivery** — Mid-page CTA after a section that demonstrated value (not before).
3. **Bottom of page** — Contact form is the final CTA. The StickyCTA bar is always "Porozmawiajmy" linking to `#contact`.
4. **One CTA per section** — Never two competing CTAs in the same visual block.

### CTA Hierarchy

| Priority | Label | Destination | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | "Porozmawiajmy o projekcie" | `#contact` | Hero, sticky bar |
| Secondary | "Zobacz [obszar]" | `#[section-id]` | Mid-page transitions |
| Tertiary | "Sprawdź szczegóły" | Subdomain URL | Pillar cards, product cards |
| Action | "Zamów [usługę]" | Form / external | Conversion-oriented sections |

## 4. Banned Words & Phrases

### NEVER use these words:

| Banned | Reason | Replace With |
|---|---|---|
| "Innowacyjne rozwiązania" | Empty buzzword | Name the specific solution |
| "Synergia" | Corporate nonsense | "Współdziałanie", "integracja" — or better, describe the mechanism |
| "Leverage" (anglicism) | Jargon, not Polish | "Wykorzystujemy", "opieramy się na" |
| "Kompleksowa obsługa" | Vague, overused | Name the scope: "od projektu po montaż", "strategia + realizacja + optymalizacja" |
| "Profesjonalny" | If you have to say it, you're not | Let the work speak — show credentials, case studies, processes |
| "Najlepszy" / "Lider" | Unsubstantiated claim | Prove it with numbers: "200+ realizacji", "15 lat doświadczenia" |
| "Unikalny" | Everything is unique if you look close enough | Describe what makes it different specifically |
| "Game-changer" / "Disruptive" | English hype words | "Zmienia podejście do...", "nowy standard w..." |
| "Rewolucyjny" | Overpromise | "Nowe podejście", "Inaczej niż dotychczas" |
| "State-of-the-art" | English + vague | Name the technology: "Next.js 16", "LangChain + OpenAI" |
| "Dynamiczny rozwój" | Cliché | Show growth: "z 0 do 50 klientów w 18 miesięcy" |
| "Pasja" / "Z pasją" | Every company claims passion | Show the result of dedication, not the emotion |

### Polish Corporate Clichés — Never Use

- "Rozwiązania szyte na miarę" → "Projekt dostosowany do Twojej infrastruktury"
- "Wychodzimy naprzeciw oczekiwaniom" → "Słuchamy i wdrażamy"
- "Klient w centrum uwagi" → "Pracujemy bezpośrednio z Twoim zespołem"
- "Indywidualne podejście" → "Jeden dedykowany Project Manager na projekt"

## 6. Tone by Section

### Hero Section
- **Tone:** Confident, aspirational, clear
- **Length:** Headline: 10-15 words. Subtitle: 20-35 words.
- **Pattern:** "Mula Group — [co robimy dla kogo]." / "[Jak to robimy — jeden akapit.]"
- **CTA:** Primary action, above fold

### About Section
- **Tone:** Grounded, human, transparent
- **Length:** 3-5 paragraphs, 40-80 words each
- **Pattern:** Origin story → what we believe → how we're different → founder context
- **No:** "Zespół ekspertów", "pasjonaci", generic "our story"

### Pillars / Services
- **Tone:** Concrete, specific, outcome-focused
- **Length:** Each pillar: Title (5 words) + Subtitle (4-8 words) + Description (15-25 words)
- **Pattern:** "[Nazwa filaru] — [jedno zdanie co robimy]. [Jedno zdanie jak to pomaga]"

### Case Studies
- **Tone:** Factual, evidence-based, no exaggeration
- **Length:** Challenge (15 words) + Solution (20 words) + Results (15 words with numbers)
- **Must include:** Specific numbers, timeframe, technologies used
- **Pattern:** "Klient X miał problem Y. Wdrożyliśmy Z. Rezultat: [konkretna liczba] w [czas]."

### Contact / CTA
- **Tone:** Inviting, low-pressure, action-oriented
- **Length:** Headline: 5-8 words. Supporting text: 10-20 words.
- **Pattern:** "[Działanie] — [co klient otrzyma]. Bez zobowiązań."

## 7. Grammar & Mechanics

### Polish Language Rules

1. **Punctuation:** Polish quotation marks: „tekst" (not "tekst" or «tekst»). Em dash: — (Alt+0151), not -- or -.
2. **Capitalization:** Only proper nouns and sentence starts. No Title Case in Polish. "Sztuczna inteligencja", not "Sztuczna Inteligencja".
3. **Formal address:** "Państwa firma", "Twój biznes", never "Twoja firma" in mixed contexts. Use "Ty" for small business owners, "Państwo" for enterprise.
4. **Numerals:** Numbers 1-9 written as words in body text ("pięć filarów"), 10+ as digits ("15 lat"). Exception: statistics always use digits ("+40% konwersji").
5. **Dates:** "5 lipca 2026", not "05.07.2026" or "2026-07-05" (body text). ISO format only in technical documentation.
6. **Abbreviations:** Spell out on first use with abbreviation in parentheses: "Sztuczna inteligencja (AI)". Then use "AI".

### English Language Rules (for international pages)

1. **Capitalization:** Sentence case for headlines. No Title Case.
2. **Quotes:** "Double quotes" (US English), 'Single quotes' for quotes within quotes.
3. **Oxford comma:** Yes — "strategy, execution, and optimization".
4. **Dates:** "July 5, 2026" in body text.

## 8. SEO Writing Guidelines

1. **Primary keyword in H1** — Always. The hero headline IS the H1.
2. **Secondary keywords in H2s** — Section headers double as SEO signals.
3. **Meta description** — 120-160 characters, includes primary keyword, CTA-adjacent.
4. **Alt text** — Every image has descriptive alt text in Polish. Include context, not just object name.
5. **Internal linking** — Link pillar cards to subdomain pages. Link case study mentions to detail pages. Link "Mula Group" in footer to main domain.
6. **URL slugs** — Polish words with diacritics stripped: `/bezpieczenstwo` not `/bezpieczeństwo`. Or use English slugs: `/security`.
7. **No keyword stuffing** — One primary keyword per section, naturally placed. Never repeat the same keyword more than twice in visible text on a page.

## 9. Content Review Checklist

Before publishing any copy, verify:

- [ ] No banned words present (see Section 4)
- [ ] Every headline follows the formula (see Section 2)
- [ ] Every CTA follows the formula (see Section 3)
- [ ] Polish diacritics are correct (ąęćłńóśźż)
- [ ] Numbers are used where possible to add credibility
- [ ] No passive voice
- [ ] Every claim is provable (if you can't back it with data, remove the claim)
- [ ] Reading level: a non-technical business owner understands every sentence
- [ ] SEO keywords present naturally (not forced)
- [ ] Mobile preview: headline doesn't break awkwardly on narrow screens
